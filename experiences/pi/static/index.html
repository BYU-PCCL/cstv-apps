Pi day is March 14 (because 3.14 are the first 3 digits of pi!).
But what is pi? And how do we compute it?
Pi is the ratio of the circumference of a circle to its diameter.
It's an irrational, transcendental number. How do you compute it?
This shows one way: by using random numbers!
We estimate pi by comparing the volume of a unit sphere to the volume of a unit cube.
But how do you calculate the volume of the sphere, without using pi?
We're generating lots of random points inside the cube.
Some points also fall inside the sphere.
We keep track of both totals.
We then estimate pi as 6 * (number inside) / total.
The more random points we use, the better our estimate of pi becomes!
(Although there are much, much better ways to compute pi!)
By the way, you can do the same thing with a circle and square.
